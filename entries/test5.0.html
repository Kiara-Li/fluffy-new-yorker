In NYC, rats are the ultimate urban survivors. They roam subway tunnels, alleyways, and trash-filled streets, scavenging for scraps. Their day is spent navigating the city’s underbelly, feasting on leftover pizza crusts, and dodging human footsteps, all while staying hidden in the shadows of this bustling metropolis.
NYC rats are expert foragers, scouring the streets and subway stations for food. They rummage through trash cans, sneak into restaurants, and feast on discarded pizza, bagels, and snacks. Their keen sense of smell helps them locate even the smallest crumbs, making them resourceful survivors in the city.
